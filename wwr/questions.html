Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is white water rafting?

    White water rafting is an exhilarating outdoor adventure activity that involves navigating a river's rapids and turbulent water using an inflatable raft. It offers a thrilling experience where participants work together as a team to paddle through the challenges of the river, providing an adrenaline rush and an opportunity to connect with nature.

  • Is white water rafting safe?

    Yes, white water rafting can be a safe adventure when conducted by experienced and certified rafting outfitters. Safety is a top priority, and guides are trained to handle various situations. Participants are provided with safety gear, and the difficulty level of the rapids is chosen based on the group's experience and comfort level.

  • What is the minimum age for rafting?

    The minimum age for white water rafting can vary depending on the location and river's difficulty level. Typically, children as young as 6 or 7 years old can participate in family-friendly rafting trips, while more challenging rapids may have a higher age requirement.

  • Do I need prior experience to go white water rafting?

    No prior experience is required for white water rafting. Rafting companies offer trips suitable for beginners, and professional guides provide safety instructions and basic training before the adventure begins. Even first-time rafters can enjoy the experience safely.

  • What is your cancellation policy?

    Cancellation within 30 days before the scheduled trip: You will receive a full refund. Cancellation within 30 days but at least 10 days before the scheduled trip: You may receive a partial refund, and any non-refundable deposits or fees will be clearly communicated. Cancellation less than 10 days before the scheduled trip: Unfortunately, we cannot offer refunds due to the advanced preparations and arrangements required for your rafting experience.
